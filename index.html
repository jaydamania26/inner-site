MY JOURNEY: FROM NOTHING TO EVERYTHING

I started my career with no shortcuts, no strong technical background, and no clear roadmap—only curiosity and the determination to make myself useful. In the early days, I was surrounded by slow, repetitive manual work and constant operational pressure. Instead of accepting it as “normal,” I began asking simple questions: Why does this take so long? Why can’t this be automated?

With zero formal automation training, I started experimenting on my own—learning from failures, breaking things, fixing them again, and slowly building solutions that actually worked. What began as small scripts to save a few minutes soon evolved into full-scale automation systems trusted by global brands like Walmart, Macy’s, and Damco.

Today, I don’t just execute tasks—I analyze workflows, identify inefficiencies, and build systems that scale. My journey from nothing to everything wasn’t overnight; it was built through consistency, self-learning, and the courage to take ownership beyond my role. I see automation not as a tool, but as a mindset: understand the problem deeply, design intelligently, and deliver results that matter.

The Foundational Years

My professional journey began at MFSG Garments, where I entered with very basic skills and no prior experience in automation or systems. My initial responsibilities were simple but demanding—handling customer orders, coordinating with workers, checking stitching quality, managing packing lists, and tracking dispatches.

Instead of seeing this as just routine work, I treated it as a learning ground. I started understanding the full production lifecycle and how data moved between teams. This is where I taught myself Excel automation, VBA, and basic scripting, experimenting after work hours and applying what I learned directly to live operations.

  • Automated daily production tracking & reduced manual calculations.
  • Played an active role in custom Mini ERP development.
  • Transformed from intern to full-time role via ownership and problem-solving.
